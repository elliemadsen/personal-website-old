virtual (im)material

unity worldbuilding

collaboration with MC Vigilante

github repository

Through the interplay of decomposition and composition, material and immaterial, this digital universe explores the glitches that emerge between the physical and virtual.

The scene objects were 3D scanned from natural specimens in the RISD Nature Lab using an Occipital Structure Sensor and imported into Unity. The landscape terrain was scanned from a hand-constructed textile, transforming stitches into rocks and fabric folds into hills. This subversion of scale invites users to encounter familiar objects through a lens of estrangement and discovery. Dynamic fractal texture mapping adds sensory complexity to obscure the objects' original forms. The Unity environment is interactive, with elements such as music, texture, object physics, and fog responding to the user's movements.

We did not aim for technical believability nor realistic representation, but rather a distinctly digital space that foregrounds abstraction and otherworldliness. We embrace the glitch as both a reminder of our earthly origins and a marker of the imperfect translation between realms.

Our process of worlding is a mode of decomposition, compressing subjective sensory forms into discrete parametrized geometries. Yet it is simultaneously an act of composition, transforming static time and place bound objects into dynamic assemblages. The representation of the objects themselves becomes an experience separate from their tactile embodiment, unrecognizable yet born of the same seed.

The world was exhibited for Digital Worlding: Terraforming Future, Fact, Fiction, and Fabulation in an installation that included the physical source materials and original music. The projection of the virtual world onto the original textile completed the cycle of physical-to-digital-to-physical transformation.
